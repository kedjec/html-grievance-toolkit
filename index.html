What do we do?

As a fulfilment to the mission of protecting and upholding the rights and welfare of every student, the STRAW Committee attends to the grievances of the students, empowers students to stand up for their rights, and capacitates every single Sillimanian for the better.

This website is one of the tangible means of achieving that goal. Established by the Silliman University Government School Year 2019-2020, this is the “Grievance Toolkit”—your one stop guide for pre-formatted letters and flowcharts to address your cases legally.

Who are we?

The Student’s Rights and Welfare (STRAW) committee is an executive committee under the president of the Silliman University Student Government (SUSG) which aims to uphold the rights and enforce the responsibilities of the students pursuant to Student’s Rights and Responsibilities under part VIII of the University Student Manual; promote student’s welfare through open discussions, awareness and legal counseling; and resolve students concerns regarding University policies, problems concerning the faculty and staff from the different departments and other related matters.

Mission

The STRAW Committee shall be:
  • Front-liners in ensuring that the SUSG protects and defends the rights of every Sillimanian student and ensure that they receive what is due to them in accordance with the University Student Manual and the 1987 Constitution.
  • Promoters of student’s welfare in every aspect – physical, mental, sociocultural, and spiritual – through programs which address the needs of the general student body.
  • Advocates for social transformation, serving as a guide for the student body in their responsive membership in the university.

Vision

A student body fully informed of their rights and equipped with the knowledge on how to protect them, a community conducive to every student’s welfare and growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

What do I do if my problem is not in the list?

Click the other issues button and it will lead allow you to send us an official message.

How many days do I have to wait before I follow up on the letter I sent?

Give it 3 working days. If there is no update, go back to the SU Grievance Center Office at SOAD.

What if I want to become anonymous?

Being anonymous will only work if the SUSG will adopt your concern or problem. Otherwise, anonymity is not going to be an ideal decision. For cases that would want to be anonymous, proceed immediately to the “other concerns” button and send the STRAW committee a message.
